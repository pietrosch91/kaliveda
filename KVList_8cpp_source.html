8 <h2>KVList</h2>
9 <h4>Extended TList class which owns its objects by default</h4>
10 This is an extended version of the ROOT TList class, with all of the extensions
11 defined in KVSeqCollection. The specificity of KVList is that it owns it objects by default:
12 any objects placed in a KVList will be deleted when the KVList is deleted, goes out of scope, or if
13 methods Clear() or Delete() are called. To avoid this, either use the
14 argument to the constructor:
15 <pre>
16 <code class=C++>KVList list_not_owner(kFALSE);//list does not own its objects
17 </code>
18 </pre>
19 or use the method SetOwner(kFALSE) to change the ownership.
20 <h3>Sorting the list</h3>
21 As TList::Sort() is defined, we implemented a KVList::Sort() method which takes exactly
22 the same argument as TList::Sort().
