38 <a name="CoordSys"></a><h2>Coordinate systems</h2>
39 
40 <a name="LAB"></a><h3>1) In laboratory frame of reference (lab-frame)</h3>
41 
42 <p>By convention, the direct coordinate system in KaliVeda
43 (called INDRA coord. syst. in <a href="#FIG1">Fig1.</a>) has the target position for its
44 center. The beam direction defines the positive (+ve) Z-axis, with respect to which
45 polar angles are measured. The +ve X-axis is taken to be vertical, and
46 azimuth angles increase when going clockwise from the +ve X-axis, looking
47 towards the +ve z direction. The angles in the INDRA spherical coord. syst. are named:
48 END_HTML
49 Begin_Latex( fontsize=20 )
50 #theta (polar angle)
51 #phi (azimuth angle)
52 End_Latex
53 BEGIN_HTML
54 theta varies between 0 and 180 degrees while phi varies between 0 and 359.999... degrees.
55 
56 <p>In the VAMOS community, the convention is a little bit different. The direct
57 coord. syst. (calle VAMOS coord. syst. in <a href="#FIG1">Fig1.</a>) is
58 also centered at the target point but the +ve X-axis is taken to be horizontal
59 and the +ve Y-axis is taken to be vertical. The +ve Z-axis, polar angles and
60 azimuth angles are defined as previously. The angles in the VAMOS spherical coord. system are
61 named:
62 END_HTML
63 Begin_Latex( fontsize=20 )
64 #theta_{l} (polar angle)
65 #phi_{l} (azimuth angle)
66 End_Latex
67 BEGIN_HTML
68 the polar angle varies between 0 to 180 degrees and the azimuth angle between -179.999... to 180 degrees. They are accessible by
69 calling the methods <a href="#KVVAMOSReconTrajectory:GetThetaL">GetThetaL</a>
70 and <a href="#KVVAMOSReconTrajectory:GetPhiL">GetPhiL</a>
71 
72 <p>For the trajectory reconstruction (see <a href="./KVVAMOSTransferMatrix.html">KVVAMOSTransferMatrix</a>)
73 , the algorithm does not used the polar coordinates but other coordinates
74 of the standard ion optics formalism. In this formalism two angles are defined:
75 <ul>
76 <li> theta_v: angle between the Z-axis and the projection of the velocity vector of the trajectory
77 on the XZ plane (symmetry plane) in the lab-frame of reference.
78 It is returned by the method
79  <a href="#KVVAMOSReconTrajectory:GetThetaV">GetThetaV</a>;</li>
80 <li> phi_v: angle between the velocity vector of the trajectory and
81 its projection on the XZ plane (symmetry plane) in the lab-frame.
82 It is returned by the method
83  <a href="#KVVAMOSReconTrajectory:GetPhiV">GetPhiV</a>.</li>
84 </ul>
85 Both angles vary between -90 to 90 degrees.
86 The relations between the cartesian coordinates of the velocity and these angles in the lab-frame are:
87 END_HTML
88 Begin_Latex( fontsize=20 )
89 v_{x} = v sin(#theta_{v}) cos(#phi_{v})
90 v_{y} = v sin(#phi_{v})
91 v_{z} = v cos(#theta_{v}) cos(#phi_{v})
92 End_Latex
93 Begin_Latex( fontsize=20 )
94 #theta_{v} = arctan( v_{x}/v_{z} )
95 #phi_{v} = arcsin( v_{y}/v )
96 End_Latex
97 BEGIN_HTML
98 
99 <a name="FP"></a><h3>2) In Focal-Plane frame of reference (FP-frame)</h3>
100 
101  The origin of the direct coord. syst. is the center of the focal plane,
102  the +ve X-axis is taken to be horizontal and the +ve Y-axis is taken to be
103  vertical as for the VAMOS coord. syst. in the lab-frame. The +ve Z-axis
104  follows the reference trajectory, polar angles and azimuth angles are defined as previously.
105 
106 The relations between the cartesian coordinates of the velocity and the different angles used in the FP-frame of reference are:
107 END_HTML
108 Begin_Latex( fontsize=20 )
109 v_{x} = v sin(#theta_{f}) cos(#phi_{f})
110 v_{y} = v sin(#phi_{f})
111 v_{z} = v cos(#theta_{f}) cos(#phi_{f})
112 End_Latex
113 Begin_Latex( fontsize=20 )
114 #theta_{f} = arctan( v_{x}/v_{z} )
115 #phi_{f} = arcsin( v_{y}/v )
116 End_Latex
117 BEGIN_HTML
118 These two angles vary between -90 to 90 degrees and are accessible by
119 calling the methods <a href="#KVVAMOSReconTrajectory:GetThetaF">GetThetaF</a>
120 and <a href="#KVVAMOSReconTrajectory:GetPhiF">GetPhiF</a>.
121 
122 <p> The intersection point of the trajectory and the focal plane is located by
123 the cartesian coordinates Xf and Yf (see <a href="#FIG1">Fig1.</a>) in cm.
124 END_HTML
125 */
